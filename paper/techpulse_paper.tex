    \begin{abstract}
    Retail investors in emerging markets are frequently disadvantaged by institutional stop-hunting mechanisms, falling victim to liquidity traps driven by distinct market structures. To address these systemic disparities, we propose a mathematically formalized quantitative trading framework that shifts from traditional technical indicators to a pure price-action quantification of Smart Money Concepts (SMC). Specifically, we mathematically model ``Liquidity Sweep'' patterns utilizing atomic OHLCV data to detect institutional intervention. To identify the optimal algorithmic core capable of providing tail-risk protection during emerging market crashes, we empirically evaluate four deep temporal architectures across Three Market Regimes (Stable, Normal, Extreme) using Vietnam's VN30 index. Experimental results demonstrate that LSTM significantly outperforms competing models during Extreme Regimes, successfully shielding capital against volatility shocks. The winning recursive model is deployed as a proof-of-concept system, providing transaction-cost-adjusted Action Scores to demonstrate practical viability for retail traders.

    \end{abstract}

    \section{Introduction}
    Retail trading is traditionally burdened by emotional biases, impulsive decision-making, and the psychological fatigue associated with continuous tape reading. Furthermore, individual investors operate under significant systemic disadvantages, routinely falling victim to stop-hunts and liquidity shakeouts strategically orchestrated by institutional participants. This dynamic is particularly pronounced in Emerging Markets, where liquidity profiles are distinct and institutional footprint impacts are highly asymmetric. While conventional algorithmic trading mitigates some emotional fatigue, it frequently relies on lagging technical indicators (e.g., Moving Averages, RSI) that generate false signals during sophisticated institutional manipulations.

    In recent years, Smart Money Concepts (SMC) have emerged as a robust paradigm to interpret true market mechanics. SMC focuses on structural elements such as order block formations, fair value gaps, and, crucially, liquidity sweeps. Despite its efficacy, SMC remains largely a discretionary framework. Integrating SMC with pure price-action quantitative algorithms represents a largely unexploited frontier in FinTech.

    Recent comprehensive reviews of machine learning and deep learning techniques in stock market prediction highlight several critical open challenges. Most notably, current models struggle with extreme financial noise, lack of interpretability, and severe vulnerability to sudden market regime shifts or "Black Swan" events \cite{mdpi2024review}. Furthermore, standard predictive architectures often fail to account for real-world transaction costs, rendering them ineffective in practical trading environments. To address these exact limitations, this study proposes a formal mathematical framework that successfully algorithmicizes Smart Money Concepts. By replacing lagging indicators with a pure price-action tensor of quantified SMC markers, and strictly optimizing for the Sharpe Ratio via Optuna, the framework explores institutional-grade, cost-adjusted analytical edges tailored for emerging market equities.

    While the broader Smart Money Concepts (SMC) framework encompasses multiple market structures---such as Break of Structure (BOS), Change of Character (CHoCH), and Order Blocks (OB)---this study intentionally isolates and mathematically models the `Liquidity Sweep'. Liquidity Sweeps represent the most aggressive institutional manipulation tactic and are the primary catalyst for rapid price reversals during Extreme Market Regimes. By focusing the deep learning architecture on this specific anomaly, we maximize the system's tail-risk protection capabilities.

    The resulting mathematical framework replaces manual chart observation with a streamlined architectural process, culminating in a proof-of-concept deployment that demonstrates the practical viability of the algorithmic core for retail investors by issuing automated alerts based on robust algorithmic setups resistant to transaction costs.

    \section{Methodology \& Feature Engineering}

    \subsection{Quantifying Smart Money Concepts: Liquidity Sweeps}
    The central premise of our feature engineering pipeline is that major directional moves are preceded by liquidity sweeps---instances where price deliberately pierces structural boundaries to trigger retail stop-loss orders before aggressively reversing.

    We mathematically model Liquidity Sweeps utilizing atomic OHLCV (Open, High, Low, Close, Volume) data. Let $L_t$ denote the local Swing Low over a historical rolling window of $w$ periods:
    \begin{equation}
    L_t = \min_{i=t-w}^{t-1} (\text{Low}_i)
    \end{equation}

    Similarly, the moving average of the trading volume $\mu_{V,t}$ is defined as:
    \begin{equation}
    \mu_{V,t} = \frac{1}{w} \sum_{i=t-w}^{t-1} \text{Volume}_i
    \end{equation}

    An institutional Liquidity Sweep ($LS_t$) is formalized as an indicator function. A bullish sweep is flagged if the current price action temporarily violates $L_t$ but subsequently mean-reverts to close above it, accompanied by an anomalous volume profile:
    \begin{equation}
    LS_t = \begin{cases} 1, & \text{if } \text{Low}_t < L_t \land \text{Close}_t > L_t \land \text{Volume}_t > k \cdot \mu_{V,t} \\ 0, & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
    \end{equation}
    This converts subjective SMC charting into a deterministic, learnable numerical feature matrix, where $k$ represents an anomalous volume threshold parameter (e.g., $k=1.5$).

    \subsection{Deep Predictive Architectures}
    The resultant pure price-action SMC dataset serves as the training corpus designed to predict post-shakeout price trajectories. We benchmark four fundamentally different architectures:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \textbf{LightGBM:} An advanced tree-based gradient boosting framework functioning as our non-temporal baseline.
        \item \textbf{LSTM:} A classical recurrent neural network evaluating longitudinal dependencies in market data.
        \item \textbf{iTransformer:} An inverted Transformer topology that separately embeds the entire historical series of individual features.
        \item \textbf{PatchTST:} A sophisticated Transformer implementation that segments temporal series into overlapping local patches to preserve localized sub-sequence patterns.
    \end{itemize}

    \subsection{Data Preprocessing and Robust Scaling}
    During the dataset construction, traditional lagging indicators (such as RSI and MACD) were explicitly excluded to test the true efficacy of SMC features. This empirical framework relies on the careful handling of 10 years of Emerging Market OHLCV data (Vietnam's VN30). The final input feature matrix comprises \textbf{7 dimensions}: five price-action channels derived from OHLCV (Open, High, Low, Close returns via \texttt{pct\_change()}, and Volume via \texttt{log1p().diff()}), augmented with two deterministic SMC indicators---the Liquidity Sweep binary flag ($LS_t \in \{0,1\}$) and its continuous strength scalar ($LS_{\text{strength},t}$). A critical preprocessing phase involves the treatment of extreme volume spikes associated with Liquidity Sweeps. Specifically, clipping infinite values to zero (which often occur due to division-by-zero errors) inadvertently blinds the AI to true institutional footprints. Instead, we implemented a ``RobustScaler'' (using Interquartile Range) to standardize the five OHLCV channels without destroying extreme accumulation outliers, while the two SMC features are passed through unchanged.

    \section{Proposed System: Architectural Core Deployment}
    The central innovation of this research is operationalizing the predictive power of the machine learning exploration into a tangible proof-of-concept deployment. 

    LSTM is implemented as the core algorithmic engine. The engine continuously processes real-time data through the SMC quantification layer, building a \textbf{7-dimensional feature tensor} $\mathbf{x}_t \in \mathbb{R}^{7}$ per trading day: $(\Delta_\text{open}, \Delta_\text{high}, \Delta_\text{low}, \Delta_\text{close}, \Delta_\text{volume}, LS_t, LS_{\text{strength},t})$. A sliding window of $T=20$ consecutive trading days forms each model input $(\mathbf{x}_{t-19}, \ldots, \mathbf{x}_t) \in \mathbb{R}^{20 \times 7}$, enabling the LSTM to decode temporal institutional footprints, ultimately outputting an \textit{Action Score} $\hat{y}_t \in [0, 1]$. Crucially, instead of adopting a static $0.5$ confidence heuristic, the system applies a Dynamically Tuned Threshold discovered specifically via the Optuna Objective Function (e.g. $0.635$ for LSTM) to ensure trades strictly execute only when mathematically favored to outpace the base $0.25\%$ transaction costs (commission + slippage).

    To eliminate manual tape reading, these Action Scores are ingested by a dual-module interface designed exclusively for retail traders:
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item \textbf{Automated Alert Mechanism:} The system evaluates Action Scores against predefined risk thresholds. When the confluence of a liquidity sweep and news sentiment yields high probabilistic confidence, the system dispatches autonomous alerts.
        \item \textbf{Integrated AI Chatbot:} Green Dragon features a sophisticated generative conversational interface. Traders can seamlessly trace the logic behind any generated alert via natural language interaction.
    \end{enumerate}

    In the deployment phase, the Green Dragon FinTech Application extends beyond pure algorithmic execution. The integrated Generative AI Chatbot acts as an educational interface for retail traders. Future iterations of the application's graphical user interface (GUI) will visually map supplementary SMC structural markers---such as Order Blocks and CHoCH---directly onto the charts. This allows the Chatbot to provide contextual, natural language explanations of the AI's Liquidity Sweep predictions, bridging the gap between complex quantitative models and retail user comprehension.

    \subsection{Research Limitations and Future Directions}
    While the pure price-action framework demonstrates robust tail-risk protection, several limitations warrant consideration. First, the mathematical feature engineering currently isolates only the ``Liquidity Sweep''. Other critical SMC structures, such as Order Blocks (OB) and Change of Character (CHoCH), are utilized heuristically in the visual frontend but are not yet formally integrated into the LSTM's multidimensional tensor space. 

    Second, integrating an LLM or Transformer-based NLP model for real-time news sentiment introduces severe computational latency and potentially disastrous slippage risks during execution. Therefore, this study is intentionally restricted to a highly optimized, low-latency $\mathbb{R}^7$ tensor of OHLCV and SMC data. Multimodal NLP fusion is explicitly reserved for future research, pending advancements in ultra-low latency inference architectures.

    Finally, the empirical evaluation is necessarily constrained. While investigating the highly liquid VN30 index provides a pristine testbed for Emerging Market dynamics, the model's generalizability to entirely different asset classes (e.g., established commodities or cryptocurrency markets) remains unverified, as institutional manipulation mechanics vary significantly across liquidity profiles.

    \section{Conclusion}
    This study proposes a mathematically formalized framework that redefines algorithmic trading for the retail sector in emerging markets by successfully quantifying deterministic Smart Money Concepts. Through the mathematical modeling of Liquidity Sweeps, this research transitions predictive engineering away from delayed technical indicators and toward the rapid structural realities of institutional stop-hunting. Our cross-validated Optuna evaluations confirmed classically scaled LSTM as the premier underlying core for this pure price-action architecture, adeptly mapping dynamic execution thresholds to overcome transaction barriers. By isolating a low-latency $\mathbb{R}^7$ feature tensor, the deployment architecture successfully mitigates the psychological detriment of active trading, thereby providing institutional-grade tail-risk protection and serving as a viable quantitative proof-of-concept for retail participants operating in volatile emerging markets.
