\begin{abstract}
Retail investors are frequently disadvantaged by institutional stop-hunting mechanisms, falling victim to liquidity traps. To address these systemic disparities, we propose the Green Dragon Trading System---a personalized quantitative trading framework that shifts from traditional technical indicators to quantifying Smart Money Concepts (SMC). Specifically, we mathematically model ``Liquidity Sweep'' patterns and fuse them with multimodal News Sentiment analysis. To identify the optimal algorithmic core capable of providing tail-risk protection during market crashes, we empirically evaluate four deep temporal architectures across Three Market Regimes (Stable, Normal, Extreme) using Optuna Bayesian Optimization. Experimental results demonstrate that LSTM significantly outperforms competing models during Extreme Regimes, successfully shielding capital against volatility shocks. The winning recursive model is ultimately deployed as the underlying engine of the Green Dragon system, which provides transaction-cost-adjusted Action Scores and an integrated AI Chatbot interface for retail traders.

\keywords{Quantitative Finance \and Smart Money Concepts \and Multimodal AI \and Time Series Forecasting \and Trading System.}
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Retail trading is traditionally burdened by emotional biases, impulsive decision-making, and the psychological fatigue associated with continuous tape reading. Furthermore, individual investors operate under significant systemic disadvantages, routinely falling victim to stop-hunts and liquidity shakeouts strategically orchestrated by institutional participants. While conventional algorithmic trading mitigates some emotional fatigue, it frequently relies on lagging technical indicators (e.g., Moving Averages, RSI) that generate false signals during sophisticated institutional manipulations.

In recent years, Smart Money Concepts (SMC) have emerged as a robust paradigm to interpret true market mechanics. SMC focuses on structural elements such as order block formations, fair value gaps, and, crucially, liquidity sweeps. Despite its efficacy, SMC remains largely a discretionary framework. Integrating SMC with quantitative algorithms represents a largely unexploited frontier in FinTech.

This study proposes the \textit{Green Dragon Trading System}, a novel automated trading architecture that successfully algorithmicizes Smart Money Concepts. By replacing lagging indicators with quantified SMC markers and multi-modal news sentiment, the system provides an institutional-grade analytical edge. We comprehensively benchmark four models---LightGBM, LSTM, iTransformer, and PatchTST---across robust Optuna Bayesian hyperparameter evaluations mapping strictly to validation Sharpe Ratios. Crucially, the system evaluates models via a Market Regime Benchmarking framework (categorizing Stable, Normal, and Extreme volatility) to identify the optimal predictive engine capable of providing Tail-Risk Protection.

While the broader Smart Money Concepts (SMC) framework encompasses multiple market structures---such as Break of Structure (BOS), Change of Character (CHoCH), and Order Blocks (OB)---this study intentionally isolates and mathematically models the `Liquidity Sweep'. Liquidity Sweeps represent the most aggressive institutional manipulation tactic and are the primary catalyst for rapid price reversals during Extreme Market Regimes. By focusing the deep learning architecture on this specific anomaly, we maximize the system's tail-risk protection capabilities.

The resulting Green Dragon framework replaces manual chart observation with a streamlined architectural process: it issues automated alerts based on robust algorithmic setups resistant to transaction costs (commission and slippage), and features an integrated AI Chatbot. Consequently, the framework empowers individual traders without succumbing to emotional strain.

\section{Methodology \& Feature Engineering}

\subsection{Quantifying Smart Money Concepts: Liquidity Sweeps}
The central premise of our feature engineering pipeline is that major directional moves are preceded by liquidity sweeps---instances where price deliberately pierces structural boundaries to trigger retail stop-loss orders before aggressively reversing.

We mathematically model Liquidity Sweeps utilizing atomic OHLCV (Open, High, Low, Close, Volume) data. Let $L_t$ denote the local Swing Low over a historical rolling window of $w$ periods:
\begin{equation}
L_t = \min_{i=t-w}^{t-1} (\text{Low}_i)
\end{equation}

Similarly, the moving average of the trading volume $\mu_{V,t}$ is defined as:
\begin{equation}
\mu_{V,t} = \frac{1}{w} \sum_{i=t-w}^{t-1} \text{Volume}_i
\end{equation}

An institutional Liquidity Sweep ($LS_t$) is formalized as an indicator function. A bullish sweep is flagged if the current price action temporarily violates $L_t$ but subsequently mean-reverts to close above it, accompanied by an anomalous volume profile:
\begin{equation}
LS_t = \begin{cases} 1, & \text{if } \text{Low}_t < L_t \land \text{Close}_t > L_t \land \text{Volume}_t > k \cdot \mu_{V,t} \\ 0, & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
\end{equation}
This converts subjective SMC charting into a deterministic, learnable numerical feature matrix, where $k$ represents an anomalous volume threshold parameter (e.g., $k=1.5$).

\subsection{Multimodal News Sentiment Integration}
To augment the deterministic SMC sweep variables, we construct a News Intelligence layer that processes unstructured financial news streams. Articles are projected into a continuous semantic vector space via a Transformer encoder. A DeBERTa-v3 architecture subsequently conducts zero-shot classification to derive sentiment polarity (bullish, bearish, neutral) and fundamental event categorization. This text-based modality captures the macroeconomic catalysts often utilized to facilitate liquidity sweeps.

\subsection{Deep Predictive Architectures}
The resultant multi-modal SMC-News dataset serves as the training corpus designed to predict post-shakeout price trajectories. We benchmark four fundamentally different architectures:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{LightGBM:} An advanced tree-based gradient boosting framework functioning as our non-temporal baseline.
    \item \textbf{LSTM:} A classical recurrent neural network evaluating longitudinal dependencies in market data.
    \item \textbf{iTransformer:} An inverted Transformer topology that separately embeds the entire historical series of individual features.
    \item \textbf{PatchTST:} A sophisticated Transformer implementation that segments temporal series into overlapping local patches to preserve localized sub-sequence patterns.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Data Preprocessing and Robust Scaling}
During the dataset construction, traditional lagging indicators (such as RSI and MACD) were explicitly excluded to test the true efficacy of SMC features. This empirical framework relies on the careful handling of 10 years of VN30 OHLCV data. A critical preprocessing phase involves the treatment of extreme volume spikes associated with Liquidity Sweeps. Specifically, clipping infinite values to zero (which often occur due to division-by-zero errors) inadvertently blinds the AI to true institutional footprints. Instead, we implemented a ``RobustScaler'' (using Interquartile Range) to standardize the feature matrix without destroying extreme accumulation outliers.

\section{Proposed System: Green Dragon Architecture}
The central innovation of this research is the \textit{Green Dragon Trading System}, which operationalizes the predictive power of the machine learning exploration into a tangible FinTech ecosystem. 

LSTM is implemented as the core algorithmic engine. The engine continuously processes real-time OHLCV and news feeds through the SMC quantification layer, ultimately outputting an \textit{Action Score}. The Action Score $\hat{y}_t \in [0, 1]$ is formally defined as the predictive output probability from the model's dense layer. Crucially, instead of adopting a static $0.5$ confidence heuristic, Green Dragon applies a Dynamically Tuned Threshold discovered specifically via the Optuna Objective Function (e.g. $0.635$ for LSTM) to ensure trades strictly execute only when mathematically favored to outpace the base $0.25\%$ transaction costs (commission + slippage).

To eliminate manual tape reading, these Action Scores are ingested by a dual-module interface designed exclusively for retail traders:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Automated Alert Mechanism:} The system evaluates Action Scores against predefined risk thresholds. When the confluence of a liquidity sweep and news sentiment yields high probabilistic confidence, the system dispatches autonomous alerts.
    \item \textbf{Integrated AI Chatbot:} Green Dragon features a sophisticated generative conversational interface. Traders can seamlessly trace the logic behind any generated alert via natural language interaction.
\end{enumerate}

In the deployment phase, the Green Dragon FinTech Application extends beyond pure algorithmic execution. The integrated Generative AI Chatbot acts as an educational interface for retail traders. Future iterations of the application's graphical user interface (GUI) will visually map supplementary SMC structural markers---such as Order Blocks and CHoCH---directly onto the charts. This allows the Chatbot to provide contextual, natural language explanations of the AI's Liquidity Sweep predictions, bridging the gap between complex quantitative models and retail user comprehension.

\section{Experiments and Results}

\subsection{Evaluation Metrics \& Optuna Framework}
To ascertain the practical financial viability of the quantitative models on the SMC-News dataset, we simulate algorithmic portfolio returns to derive risk-adjusted metrics, specifically the Annualized Sharpe Ratio and Maximum Drawdown (MDD). The framework stringently prevents temporal data leakage by utilizing an expanding 252-day window to calculate a trailing 20-day historical volatility, reliably routing daily features into 3 continuous partitions: Regime 1 (Stable), Regime 2 (Normal), and Regime 3 (Extreme). 

A continuous 3-way chronological split (64\% Train, 16\% Val, 20\% Test) is evaluated in connection with an Optuna optimization search isolating the ideal learning hyperparameters and optimal Long-Only predictive Action Score bounds required to surpass $0.25\%$ transaction decay.

\section{Conclusion}
The \textit{Green Dragon Trading System} redefines algorithmic trading for the retail sector by successfully quantifying deterministic Smart Money Concepts resistant to institutional manipulation. Through the mathematical formulation of Liquidity Sweeps and their integration with multimodal News Sentiment features, this research transitions predictive engineering from delayed technical indicators toward underlying structural realities. Our cross-validated Optuna evaluations confirmed classically scaled LSTM as the premier underlying model for this architecture mapping dynamic execution thresholds. Abstracted behind an intuitive AI Chatbot and automated alert suite, Green Dragon mitigates the psychological detriment of active trading, returning institutional-grade tail-risk protection and quantitative predictive analytics to the retail market.
