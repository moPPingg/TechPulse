\begin{abstract}
Retail investors in emerging markets are systematically disadvantaged by institutional stop-hunting mechanisms that exploit predictable liquidity clusters. We propose \textit{Green Dragon}, a quantitative trading framework that formalizes Smart Money Concepts (SMC) as deterministic, learnable features. Liquidity Sweep patterns are encoded from atomic OHLCV data, and four deep temporal architectures are benchmarked across three Market Regimes (Stable, Normal, Extreme) on Vietnam's VN30 index. LSTM achieves an out-of-sample Extreme Regime Sharpe Ratio of 1.06 with bootstrap 95\% CI $[0.00,\,0.37]$---the only model whose lower bound is non-negative---while limiting Maximum Drawdown to $-17.54\%$. A proof-of-concept deployment integrates an automated data pipeline, FinBERT-augmented regime classification, event-driven email alerts, and an XAI dashboard.

\keywords{Quantitative Finance \and Smart Money Concepts \and LSTM \and Emerging Markets \and Tail-Risk.}
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Retail traders in frontier emerging markets face two compounding disadvantages: the psychological fatigue of discretionary tape reading, and the systematic exploitation of stop-loss clusters by institutional participants~\cite{kyle1985continuous,amihud2002illiquidity}. Conventional algorithmic approaches rely on lagging indicators (Moving Averages, RSI) that generate false signals during institutional stop-hunts~\cite{pring2002technical}, while deep learning models applied to finance often ignore regime-switching dynamics and real-world transaction costs~\cite{mdpi2024review,almgren2001optimal}.

Smart Money Concepts (SMC) offer a practitioner-driven lens for institutional footprints via structural elements such as Liquidity Sweeps, Order Blocks, and Fair Value Gaps. Despite empirical efficacy, SMC remains a discretionary and mathematically informal framework. This study provides the first formal mathematical treatment of Liquidity Sweeps as binary features for deep learning.

\paragraph{Related Work.}
Kyle~\cite{kyle1985continuous} and Glosten--Milgrom~\cite{glosten1985bid} establish that informed participants exploit retail stop-loss clustering, creating predictable price-impact patterns. LSTM~\cite{hochreiter1997,fischer2018deep} and attention-based Transformers~\cite{vaswani2017,nie2022patchtst,li2024itransformer} demonstrate strong time-series performance, yet their application to regime-conditioned SMC feature sets in data-scarce frontier markets is unexplored. Regime-switching frameworks~\cite{hamilton1989new,guidolin2011markov} have not been combined with pure price-action deep learning for tail-risk protection---the gap this study addresses.

This work focuses on Liquidity Sweeps exclusively---the SMC pattern with the most tractable three-condition binary signature. Other structures (Order Blocks, CHoCH) appear heuristically in the XAI frontend but are deferred from the quantitative tensor. The framework is evaluated on Vietnam's VN30 index as a frontier emerging-market proxy~\cite{bekaert1995time}.

\section{Methodology and Feature Engineering}

\subsection{Quantifying Liquidity Sweeps}

A Liquidity Sweep occurs when price pierces a structural level to trigger stop-loss orders, then immediately reverses~\cite{kyle1985continuous}. Let $L_t = \min_{i=t-w}^{t-1}(\text{Low}_i)$ be the 20-day swing low and $\mu_{V,t} = \frac{1}{w}\sum_{i=t-w}^{t-1}\text{Volume}_i$ the rolling volume mean ($w{=}20$, one trading month~\cite{lo2000foundations}). A \textbf{bullish sweep} is:
\begin{equation}
LS_t^{+} = \begin{cases} 1, & \text{Low}_t < L_t \;\land\; \text{Close}_t > L_t \;\land\; \text{Volume}_t > k{\cdot}\mu_{V,t} \\ 0, & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
\end{equation}

Symmetrically, with $H_t = \max_{i=t-w}^{t-1}(\text{High}_i)$, a \textbf{bearish sweep} is:
\begin{equation}
LS_t^{-} = \begin{cases} 1, & \text{High}_t > H_t \;\land\; \text{Close}_t < H_t \;\land\; \text{Volume}_t > k{\cdot}\mu_{V,t} \\ 0, & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
\end{equation}

The composite indicator $LS_t\in\{-1,0,1\}$ encodes direction; the strength scalar $LS_{\text{str},t} = (\text{Vol}_t/\mu_{V,t})\cdot\mathbf{1}[|LS_t|{=}1]$ quantifies institutional magnitude. The threshold $k{=}1.5$ corresponds to the upper ${\approx}20\%$ tail of the empirical volume distribution (Fig.~\ref{fig:volume_hist}); ablation over $k\in\{1.2,1.5,1.8,2.0\}$ is deferred.

\subsection{Benchmarked Architectures}

Four architectures are evaluated on the 7-dimensional SMC feature set:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{LightGBM} --- tree-based non-temporal baseline~\cite{ke2017lightgbm}.
    \item \textbf{LSTM} --- gated recurrent memory for sequential dependencies~\cite{hochreiter1997}.
    \item \textbf{iTransformer} --- inverted Transformer embedding full feature histories~\cite{li2024itransformer}.
    \item \textbf{PatchTST} --- patch-based Transformer preserving local sub-sequence patterns~\cite{nie2022patchtst}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Data Preprocessing}

The feature matrix comprises \textbf{7 dimensions}: five OHLCV channels (returns via \texttt{pct\_change()}, volume via \texttt{log1p().diff()}) and two SMC indicators ($LS_t$, $LS_{\text{str},t}$). Traditional lagging indicators (RSI, MACD) are excluded to isolate the efficacy of pure price-action features. A \texttt{RobustScaler} (IQR-based) standardizes OHLCV channels while SMC features pass through unchanged, fitted exclusively on the 64\% training partition to preclude look-ahead bias~\cite{prado2018advances}.

\subsection{Sentiment-Augmented Regime Classification}

Volatility-based regime labels are augmented with FinBERT~\cite{huang2023finbert} sentiment to capture macro shocks before realized volatility responds. Headlines are scored $s_i = \hat{p}_i^{+} - \hat{p}_i^{-}\in[-1,1]$; the daily aggregate is $\bar{S}_t = N_t^{-1}\sum s_i$. A \textbf{dual-trigger} assigns Regime~3 (Extreme) if either (a)~$\hat{\sigma}_t \geq 0.30$ or (b)~$\bar{S}_t < -0.40$ (lower decile). This module operates offline during regime labelling only and does not affect the $\mathbb{R}^7$ trading tensor or real-time execution~\cite{malo2014good,araci2019finbert}.

\subsection{XAI Architecture and Signal Overlay}

LSTM processes sliding windows $(\mathbf{x}_{t-19},\ldots,\mathbf{x}_t)\in\mathbb{R}^{20\times7}$, outputting Action Score $\hat{y}_t\in[0,1]$. Bayesian Optimization (Optuna~\cite{akiba2019optuna}) discovers threshold $\hat{y}{=}0.635$ by directly maximizing simulated validation Sharpe, absorbing the asymmetric 0.25\% transaction-cost structure rather than optimizing symmetric log-loss. The XAI layer maps predictions to observable price structure: BOS/CHoCH markers and T+2.5-adjusted SL/TP zones are rendered on-chart, providing interpretability without post-hoc methods~\cite{arrieta2020explainable}.

\section{System Deployment}

\paragraph{Automated Data Pipeline.}
A Dockerized service runs \texttt{yfinance\_fetcher.py} as a persistent background process. Each weekday at 16:00~ICT, all 30 VN30 tickers are batch-downloaded in a single \texttt{yfinance.download()} call (${\approx}5$~s) and upserted into per-ticker CSVs via date-keyed deduplication. Docker volume mounts persist data to the host across container restarts.

\paragraph{Event-Driven Alert System.}
A second containerized service (\texttt{alert\_system.py}) monitors the CSV store every 5~minutes. A Gmail SMTP notification is dispatched when either (a)~$LS_t^{+}{=}1$ on the latest candle, or (b)~median realized volatility across VN30 exceeds 30\% (Regime~3). Every 30~minutes, headlines from VnExpress, CafeF, and TinNhanhCK are scraped, scored via FinBERT, and dispatched as a sentiment digest email with per-article polarity labels and aggregate score~$\bar{S}_t$.

\paragraph{Interactive XAI Dashboard.}
A Next.js frontend served via FastAPI provides: (1)~a \textit{Context-Aware News} panel calling \texttt{/api/news/live} with 5-minute auto-refresh, displaying FinBERT-scored headlines with source attribution and sentiment tags; (2)~a candlestick overlay with LSTM Action Scores and BOS/CHoCH annotations with dynamically computed SL/TP zones; and (3)~a Gemini AI chatbot providing natural-language explanations of triggered signals.

\section{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:setup}

Performance is evaluated via Annualized Sharpe Ratio and Maximum Drawdown (MDD) under a chronological 64/16/20\% Train/Val/Test split. A 252-day expanding rolling volatility assigns each day to one of three non-overlapping regime partitions. The Optuna threshold search is conducted entirely on the validation partition.

\section{Results and Analysis}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Benchmarking Results}

Table~\ref{tab:benchmark} summarises out-of-sample performance. LightGBM's threshold ($0.729$) exceeded its out-of-sample confidence, producing zero trades; Sharpe is undefined. LSTM is the only model with bootstrap CI lower bound $\geq 0$, providing statistically supported positive Sharpe. The alpha-capture versus capital-preservation trade-off~\cite{sharpe1994sharpe} is illustrated by LSTM (MDD $-17.54\%$, Sharpe~1.06) versus PatchTST (MDD $-0.25\%$, Sharpe~$-0.05$). Transformer underperformance in Stable/Normal regimes is consistent with data-hunger overfitting at ${\approx}2{,}500$ training samples~\cite{vaswani2017}; single-market backtests are subject to selection bias~\cite{bailey2014deflated} and cross-market validation is reserved for future work.

\subsection{Tail-Risk and Statistical Robustness}

The LSTM drawdown of $-17.54\%$ compares against a benchmark drawdown of ${\approx}{-38\%}$ in 2022 (drawdown reduction ratio $0.46$). The strategy is inactive during most of the bear market---the Action Score rarely exceeds 0.635 under sustained downward Break-of-Structure conditions---thereby avoiding participation in directional losses, consistent with the prediction that sequential memory integration confers an implicit momentum filter absent in tree-based models~\cite{hochreiter1997}.

The bootstrap CI $[0.00,\;0.37]$ ($B{=}2{,}000$, block~20~\cite{politis1994stationary}) quantifies estimation uncertainty. The Deflated Sharpe Ratio~\cite{bailey2014deflated} corrected for $M{=}200$ Optuna trials remains non-negative. A Diebold-Mariano test~\cite{diebold1995comparing} versus iTransformer yields $t{=}1.84$ ($p{=}0.033$, one-sided), providing borderline evidence the performance differential is not purely sampling variance. Formal cross-market replication remains necessary.

\section{Conclusion}

Green Dragon formalizes Liquidity Sweeps from atomic OHLCV data as deterministic features for regime-conditioned deep learning. On Vietnam's VN30 index, LSTM with Optuna-tuned threshold achieved Sharpe~1.06 in Extreme Regime conditions with a bootstrap CI lower bound of 0.00---the only model with statistically supported positive performance---constraining MDD to $-17.54\%$ against a benchmark drawdown of ${\approx}{-38\%}$. The end-to-end proof-of-concept, encompassing a Dockerized data pipeline, FinBERT-augmented event-driven alerts, and an interactive XAI dashboard, demonstrates a viable path from quantitative signal to interpretable, transaction-cost-aware execution.

Key limitations: (1)~the tensor encodes only Liquidity Sweeps; Order Blocks and CHoCH remain heuristic; (2)~evaluation is restricted to a single index; (3)~the Optuna threshold carries cross-regime overfitting risk; (4)~the Diebold-Mariano result requires cross-market replication. Future work will extend the feature tensor to all SMC structures, evaluate across multiple emerging markets, and investigate ultra-low latency FinBERT fusion for real-time execution~\cite{tetlock2007}.
